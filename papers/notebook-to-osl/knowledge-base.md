# Knowledge base

This file tracks the knowledge needed to turn the idea into a publishable paper.

## Topic areas to review

### Digital Twin development

Questions:

- How do DT papers describe early-stage knowledge capture?
- How are informal operational descriptions transformed into DT models, artefacts, dashboards, or services?
- Where is operator knowledge treated as a first-class input?

Needed references:

- Digital Twin definitions and architectures
- Human-in-the-loop Digital Twins
- Digital Twins in CNC/manufacturing
- DT requirements and conceptual modelling

### Operator knowledge and expertise

Questions:

- How is tacit operator knowledge described in manufacturing literature?
- How do operators recognize abnormal states?
- How are sensory cues such as sound, vibration, smell, timing, or surface finish discussed?

Needed references:

- tacit knowledge
- situated expertise
- shop-floor knowledge
- operator decision-making
- human factors in manufacturing

### Situated learning and apprenticeship

Questions:

- What theoretical basis supports learning by being trained in practice?
- How can apprenticeship-like participation be legitimate as a research method?
- How can observations during training be transformed into analyzable data?

Needed references:

- situated learning
- apprenticeship
- ethnographic field notes
- contextual inquiry
- participant observation

### Requirements elicitation

Questions:

- How are requirements elicited in complex socio-technical systems?
- What methods exist for moving from field observations to structured requirements?
- How are uncertainty, rationale, and exceptions preserved?

Needed references:

- requirements elicitation
- contextual design
- scenario-based requirements
- rationale capture
- human-centred design for engineering systems

### Knowledge representation and modelling

Questions:

- How can notes be transformed into semi-formal or formal representations?
- What concepts are needed before OSL formalization?
- How can OSL candidates preserve context and uncertainty?

Needed references:

- knowledge representation
- conceptual modelling
- domain-specific languages
- SysML v2 or model-based systems engineering, if relevant

## Empirical material needed

Minimum viable empirical material:

1. One situated training session or realistic pilot.
2. A set of raw notes.
3. Annotated versions of selected notes.
4. A small set of OSL candidate fragments.
5. Operator or domain-expert validation of at least some transformed notes.

## Sensitive-data precautions

Avoid storing:

- proprietary production parameters
- customer names
- machine programs
- business-sensitive process details
- identifiable operator statements without consent

Prefer storing:

- generalized CNC examples
- anonymized note fragments
- abstracted operator cues and actions
- sanitized process descriptions
