# Literature foundation for Notebook-to-OSL

This file records the core result of the deep literature review and how it strengthens the paper.

## Main positioning

Notebook-to-OSL should be framed as a **hybrid elicitation-and-transformation method** for human-in-the-loop Digital Twin development.

The method is not just note-taking and not a complete OSL language paper. Its contribution is the traceable transformation from situated operator-training notes into validated, model-ready operator-strategy fragments.

## Literature streams to build on

| Literature stream | Role in the paper |
|---|---|
| Digital Twin manufacturing literature | Establishes the architecture/data/model baseline and shows the gap around operator knowledge elicitation. |
| Human-in-the-loop and human-centred Digital Twins | Supports treating operators as knowledge-bearing participants rather than only end users. |
| Situated learning and cognitive apprenticeship | Justifies operator training as a legitimate elicitation setting. |
| Contextual inquiry and contextual design | Supports in-context observation, partnership, interpretation checks, and preserving field context. |
| Cognitive task analysis / ACTA / critical decision method | Supports probing cues, difficult judgments, expert-novice differences, exceptions, and escalation logic. |
| Requirements engineering and SEBoK | Supports traceability, rationale, risk, source, ownership, status, and validation. |
| EARS, SysML v2, and semantic Digital Twin modelling | Supports downstream model-readiness and transformation into requirements, model elements, rules, or knowledge structures. |

## Stronger method identity

The method now has three layers:

1. **Contextual/apprenticeship front end**  
   The researcher learns beside the operator and captures low-friction digital field notes.

2. **CTA-style clarification layer**  
   Selected notes are probed for cues, thresholds, alternatives, exceptions, novice pitfalls, and escalation paths.

3. **Requirements/MBSE back end**  
   Validated fragments are given traceability, rationale, status, risk, and a model-ready target.

## Recommended pilot evaluation

A credible minimum pilot should include:

- one focused CNC training scenario;
- one operator or domain expert;
- 20--40 raw notes;
- 8--15 annotated strategy fragments;
- 4--8 validated model-ready candidates;
- transformation metrics;
- operator validation feedback;
- a reflection log on researcher interpretation and confidentiality.

## Validation statuses

Use these validation outcomes:

- `accepted`
- `corrected`
- `narrowed`
- `rejected`
- `sensitive`
- `model-ready`

## Key paper claim

> Human-in-the-loop Digital Twins require not only connected data and models, but also explicit methods for preserving situated operator reasoning as traceable, validated, model-ready knowledge.
