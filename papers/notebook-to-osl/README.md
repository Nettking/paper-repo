# Notebook-to-OSL

## Working title

**From Apprenticeship Notes to Operator Strategy Models: Situated Elicitation for Digital Twin Development**

Alternative title:

**Notebook-to-OSL: Capturing Operator Strategies during CNC Operator Training**

## Core idea

A researcher or engineer stands beside a CNC operator during situated training. The operator is instructed to teach the researcher how the process is understood and handled in practice. The researcher takes short notes on a laptop during the training session. Afterward, each note can be selected in a lightweight application and transformed into an annotated operator-strategy fragment. These fragments can later become candidate constructs in an Operator Strategy Language (OSL).

## Central claim

Digital Twin development often assumes that relevant operational knowledge can be elicited through interviews, workshops, or access to machine data. This paper argues that some operator strategies are only visible when the researcher is trained in the practice itself. The contribution is a method and tool-supported workflow for moving from situated training notes to structured OSL candidates.

## Intended contribution

1. A situated elicitation method for collecting operator strategy knowledge during training.
2. A note-to-strategy annotation schema.
3. A workflow from raw laptop notes to OSL candidate fragments.
4. An illustrative CNC case showing how observations, cues, actions, uncertainty, and rationale can be captured.
5. A discussion of how this workflow supports later Digital Twin modelling.
