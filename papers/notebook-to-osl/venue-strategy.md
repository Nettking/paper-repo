# Venue strategy

## Plan A: Advanced Engineering Informatics

Best fit if the paper is framed as engineering knowledge capture, Digital Twin development, formalization, and tool-supported transformation from situated notes to model candidates.

Suggested framing:

> A tool-supported situated elicitation method for capturing operator strategies during CNC operator training and transforming raw field notes into candidate formal representations for Digital Twin development.

Why this venue fits:

- engineering knowledge representation
- engineering informatics
- Digital Twin development
- knowledge capture and reuse
- method + tool support
- formalization of informal engineering knowledge

Risk:

- The paper must show a clear informatics contribution, not only a qualitative field observation.
- The transformation workflow and annotation schema must be precise.

## Plan B: Computers in Industry

Good backup if the paper is framed around practical industrial ICT support, lightweight tooling, and implementation in CNC/DT development.

Suggested framing:

> A lightweight annotation tool and workflow for capturing operator knowledge in CNC Digital Twin development.

Risk:

- Needs enough industrial relevance and practical detail.

## Plan C: Journal of Manufacturing Systems

Good option only if the CNC/manufacturing-system contribution becomes strong.

Suggested framing:

> Situated operator training as a method for deriving human-in-the-loop decision support requirements for CNC Digital Twins.

Risk:

- May expect stronger manufacturing-system-level evaluation or integration with machine data.

## Plan D: Requirements Engineering

Good option if the paper becomes primarily about eliciting, structuring, validating, and formalizing requirements for software-intensive Digital Twin systems.

Risk:

- Need stronger connection to requirements engineering theory and validation.

## Fallback: workshop or conference

Use if the concept, schema, and prototype exist but empirical material is still too thin for a journal paper.

Possible workshop framing:

> Notebook-to-OSL: A Position Paper on Capturing Operator Strategies during CNC Training

Workshop version can include:

- concept
- prototype workflow
- annotation schema
- one or two illustrative examples
- planned evaluation
